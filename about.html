Glenn Lopez | about me

Who am I?

I'm Glenn, an Engineering Technologist with a history of working in Aerospace & Defense, Aviation, Test engineering, and Electronic manufacturing. I'm a natural-born problem solver, eager to learn, quick to master, and capable of rapidly adapting to changing situations.

As a self-taught programmer, maintainer, and enthusiast of all things open-sourced, I moonlight as an embedded systems designer, cultivating my passion for programming and hardware design, while perfecting my craft in software development.

Hobbies & Interests

When I'm not converting coffee into code for fun and profit, you'll find me working on my motorbike, fpv drones, robots, dabbling in cybersecurity, experimenting with laser cutters, 3D printers, CNC machines, getting my hands dirty with graphics design, and perfecting my lock-picking and speed cubing skills.

My Knowledge & Experience Spectrum

🧑‍💻

Software Development

Started with HTML, CSS, Javascript in 2006, branching out to C, C++, C#, and Python in 2010-2018. Today, my favourite language is C# paired with .Net Framework.

🤖

Embedded Systems Design

Arduino was my first love, eventually branching out to MSP430, PSoC4, LPC, even getting into Xilinx. These days, I mainly use ESP32 and STM32 for my projects.

⚙️

Mechanical CAD

SolidWorks was my first taste into CAD, these days I use Fusion360 for the four 3D printers (3 FDM, 1 SLA), 50 Watt laser cutter, and CNC Router I own to bring my designs to life.

🛩️

Private Pilot Training

My first solo flight happened on December 19th, 2006. From there my love for flight took off, getting into Aircraft Maintenance, Aerospace Manufacturing, Sky-diving, and getting certified as a drone operator.

👷‍♂️

Certified Drone Operator

FPV drones were all things I enjoy combined into one hobby. I had to get certified if I wanted test the boundaries of my home built fpv drones (flying at 150km/h or over 3km high).

📡

HAM Radio Operator (pending)

Having an aeronautical restricted radio operator license wasn't enough. I also needed a Ham Radio license if I was gonna broadcast my fpv drones at 1-5 watts.

💡

Electronic CAD

Circuit Maker got me into Altium, but ultimately fell in-love with KiCAD. These days, I mainly use KiCAD for open-source projects, and Altium for something more "closed-source".

🖥️

Computer Repair

My first real hobby started in 2005 after getting a certificate in computer repair. From there, I nerded out on specs, latest hardware, built my first gaming rig, I even ran Crysis on it at 1080p (medium settings).

🧠

AI & ML

Like you, and everyone else... I jumped on the hype train and built my first machine learning model in python to play flappy birds. These days, Im digging my self deeper and deeper in ML. I'll be using NVIDIA's Jetson platform to teach myself AI & Data Science.

🎒

Always learning

No matter, where or when. I always make time for learning. My favorite MOOC platform is Coursera, edX, Harvard open courses, and MIT OpenCourseWare.
